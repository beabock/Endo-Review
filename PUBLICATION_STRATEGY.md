## Manuscript Framework for Top-Tier Submission

### Title Options (Nature/Science style)
1. "Fungal endophytes: A universal plant symbiosis"
2. "No plant species lacks fungal endophytes: Evidence for biological universality"
3. "Universal fungal colonization across the plant kingdom"

### Abstract Framework (150 words max for Nature)
```
The relationship between plants and fungal endophytes has been documented across 
numerous plant species, leading to claims of universality. However, no systematic 
analysis has tested whether ANY plant species lacks fungal endophytes. Here we 
analyze [X] studies spanning [Y] years and [Z] plant species using automated 
citation network analysis and comprehensive literature review. We find that 
NO plant species has been definitively shown to lack fungal endophytes, despite 
extensive sampling across major plant lineages. Citation network analysis reveals 
that universality claims have propagated through the literature without 
comprehensive testing. Our findings establish fungal endophytes as truly universal 
plant symbionts and provide the first systematic evidence supporting this 
fundamental ecological principle. These results reshape our understanding of 
plant biology and suggest that the plant-fungal partnership represents one of 
the most successful symbioses in nature.
```

### Main Text Structure for Nature/Science

#### Introduction (~500 words)
- Plant-microbe symbioses as fundamental to life
- Endophyte ubiquity claims in literature
- Lack of systematic testing
- "Here we provide the first comprehensive test..."

#### Results (~1500 words)
- Systematic literature analysis
- Zero counter-examples found
- Citation network reveals claim propagation
- Phylogenetic breadth of evidence

#### Discussion (~800 words)
- Ecological implications of true universality
- Evolutionary significance
- Methodological contributions
- Future research directions

#### Methods (~200 words in main text)
- Brief overview, full details in Extended Data

### Extended Data & Supplementary Material
- Complete methodology
- Citation analysis pipeline
- Full dataset
- Reproducible analysis code
- Detailed statistical analyses
